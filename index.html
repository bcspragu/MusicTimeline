Martha Graham's Lamentation (1943)

A rebellion against freedom and fluidity of motion.

Her emphasis on tight, locked motions would prove to be the foundation for many modern styles of hip-hop and electronic-inspired dance. Based on bondage and chains, Martha Graham's movements are reminiscent of slavery and captivity, a nod to African-American heritage.

Daft Punk's Technologic (2005)

Confined by Technology.

Daft Punk satirizes the direction of technology with quick, punchy commands. They tell audiences to "lock" it and "zip" it, showing them the ridiculous tendencies of man towards the subjugation of others.

Jabbawockeez (2012)

The empitome of restrictive dance.

An evolution of the punchy beats displayed by Daft Punk, the Jabbawockeez are a physical, fluid manifestation of the music. They display incredible control over their bodies and their art form. Their movements are precise and clean, without freedom.

Banksy plays on how technology obstructs our most basic needs.

Technology has infiltrated our lives to such a point where it interferes with our abilities to experience love. It's reached beyond a point where we restrict our dance motions and our beats and into a realm where it's restricted our emotional capacities.
